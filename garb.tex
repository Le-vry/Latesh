\begin{abstract}
This study investigates the effectiveness of parallel
database calls as a method for improving loading times
in a Flutter/Firebase mobile application. Measurements
were conducted on a sample mobile app with interleaved testing of both parallel and sequential network calls, with a focus on loading times as the
key performance metric. The measurements were performed under controlled
conditions, with a total of 150 datapoints each for the profile and leaderboard
pages. %LûÊgg till att det ûÊr per db storlek
These tests were performed with varying database sizes (100, 500, 1000
users) to evaluate the method's effectiveness under different load conditions.
The results indicate a significant reduction in loading times after the
implementation of parallel database calling with the improvent being in the
range of 102 ms to 219 ms depending on the page with an observed
improvement between 18\% and 31\% depending on the page and database size.
Statiscal analasys shows that the improvement is statistically significant for
both pages, with no notable deviations in relation to database size.
These findings suggest that parallel database calls can be an effective method
for improving loading times in flutter/firebase mobile applications. The results
should however be interpreted with caution, as the study was conducted on a
single application and under specific conditions. Further research is needed to generalize these findings to other applications and contexts.
\end{abstract}
